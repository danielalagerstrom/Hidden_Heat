Welcome to Sompasauna: Helsinki's community-driven sauna experience

Nestled in the heart of Verkkosaari, Sompasauna offers a unique, 24/7 sauna experience where the warmth of the sauna is matched only by the spirit of community. Here, visitors play a hands-on role in maintaining and operating the sauna, creating a welcoming environment built on collaboration and voluntary work. With no staff, no ticketing system, and no reservations needed, Sompasauna is open to everyone. Simply show up and enjoy the relaxing heat of three distinct saunas and a year-round swimming area. Whether you’re unwinding after a busy day or connecting with like-minded sauna lovers, you’ll feel right at home.

The saunas are mixed, with the option of wearing a swimsuit at your discretion. Changing rooms and showers are not available, keeping the experience simple and grounded in tradition. Participation is encouraged, and instructions on how to contribute are posted on the notice boards. Sompasauna is a place to relax, rejuvenate, and embrace the Finnish sauna culture, all while being part of a community that works together to keep the spirit of the sauna alive. Join us, no reservation required—just bring yourself and your appreciation for sauna tradition.

What makes it a hidden gem:

Sompasauna is one of the few public saunas in Finland that operates with a DIY, communal and open-access philosophy. Its location offers a peaceful escape from the urban hustle while still being easily accessible from the city. Sompasauna is also free of charge!

Fun Fact:

Sompasauna was founded in early summer 2011 after a few men from Helsinki found an abandoned wood-burning stove. The men came up with a use for the stove by building a “shack sauna” on the wasteland of the former harbour, with their own permits, for everyone to use freely. The founders of the sauna were not using the sauna themselves, but in the first year a few hundred sauna lovers found their way there.
